▸ How risk is determined & data notes

Each active onboarding milestone (status in progress) is scored from its ClickUp signals: Product RAG, Customer Status, Delay & Adoption Risk, Product Phase, days in onboarding, recorded go-live / first-value dates, and the parent engagement RAG. ClickUp fields are treated as evidence, not absolute truth — a delay flag or passed target date on a project already moving through a later phase is monitored, not escalated.

High

Product RAG Red; or an Amber project with an active blocker, escalation, or a materially slipped timeline; or any project (including Green RAG) that is explicitly stalled, on hold, not engaged, or carries a serious adoption risk (product gap, lost sponsor, competitor, mis-sold).

Medium

Amber RAG; or an early-phase project that is aging without a go-live, carries a delay dependency, has a passed target go-live, or a slow customer.

Green-RAG High Risk

A project can read Green in ClickUp yet be genuinely at risk (e.g. on hold or a product gap). The KPI counts these so the field-level RAG can be brought back in line; each appears in the list with its reason under "Why flagged".

Product ARR mapping

Product ARR sums every relevant column for the product line — e.g. Experimentation = web + feature + platform + add-ons. Where a line has no standalone ARR (services, overlays, bundled analytics) Product ARR is $0 and Account ARR (total_arr) carries the account value. This corrected 31 projects that previously displayed $0.
